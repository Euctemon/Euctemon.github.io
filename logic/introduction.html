Mathematical logic

The following pages are about mathematical logic. During my engineering studies, I wasn't concerned about logic at all. After I finished mechanical engineering, I went to study pure mathematics. For my bachelor thesis I chose to prove a theorem from algebra in a theorem prover. Theorem provers are programs for proving mathematical statements, and they rely heavily on logic. I got acquainted with some not-so-known types of logic systems and how they are used in theorem proving. I soon discovered that I had not understood mathematical logic at all. Why is first order logic so ubiquous? Why do we care about truth tables and how do they interact with propositional logic? Why do most theorem provers use higher order logic? There are so many interesting question connected to logic. I don't think my undestanding got much better since I started learning about these things. Nevertheless, I wanted to put down some of my thoughts on logic here, hoping that writing about it would make at least some ideas clear.
