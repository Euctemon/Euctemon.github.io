Introduction

I never though I would be interested in logic in slightest. Do you need logic for ordinary life? Do you get in touch with logic at all? The interesting thing is that the word logical is used by people quite often. Some people might say they are logical thinkers, possibly as opposed to erratic or chaotic. People might say that something is illogical. If you look at synonyms, you might find words like inconsistent, absurd, false, implausible, unreasonable or even unwise. In a book Being logical the author says that logical people are those who are attentive, get facts straight, ensure their ideas are clear, are mindful of the origins of ideas, match ideas to facts and words to ideas. They also communicate effectively and avoid vague or ambiguous language.

When studying pure mathematics you come across mathematical logic. This branch of mathematics is concerned about the use of logic in mathematics. Mathematics is not the only place where one can use logic. For example philosphers use logic and it could be argued that for them logic is even more important. It might come as a surprise that a lot of mathematicians do not know about logic as much as you would think. Personally during my undergraduate studies I had two courses on logic. One was an introductory course and the other one was on the computational aspects of logic, or in others words, how can we do logic on a computer. Apart from that, we were taught how to deal with quantifiers, how to structure a proof by contradiction and were taught few other tools applicable in mathematics. Mathematical logic is a vast subject so the thought of mastering logic before doing mathematics at all is rather unfeasible.

The purpose of these notes is to organize my notes I collected throughout my studies and maybe get a little farther in understanding logic. Apart from lecture notes, this text is heavily influenced by an awesome book called Mathematical logic and computation by Jeremy Avigad.
